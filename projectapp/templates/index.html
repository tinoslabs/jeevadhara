Who We Are

Jeevadhara HMC is a homoeopathic treatment centre,, dedicated to providing holistic healthcare to patients. Founded by Dr. (Sr.) Sherine MSJ, BHMS, MD, the centre follows a holistic approach, guided by the motto of Love serves, inspired by the master healer Jesus.

Dr. (Sr.) Sherine MSJ, the chief physician, has completed her post-graduation in Materia Medica from the Rajive Gandhi University of Health Sciences in Bangalore.

Our Congregation

The congregation of the Medical Sisters of St. Joseph was founded in 1944 by the late Rev. Msgr. Joseph. C. Panjikaran at Dharmagiri Kothamangalam. The congregation aims to provide medical facilities to the poor, downtrodden, and helpless people of Kerala.

Msgr. Panjikaran, the founder, was born on September 10th, 1888. Deeply touched by the miseries of the poor, especially the sick, he envisioned the sick bed as the best place to experience the love of God.

Discover the Healing Power of Homeopathy



Homeopathy is a system of medicine that treats individuals with highly diluted substances, primarily in tablet form, aiming to trigger the body's natural healing mechanisms. Practitioners select medicines based on the patient's symptoms and their holistic assessment of the individual's health. The fundamental principle of homeopathy is "like cures like." This means a substance that causes symptoms in a healthy person can treat similar symptoms in someone who is unwell. For example, coffea, a remedy made from coffee, might be used to alleviate insomnia.

▶️ Samuel Hahnemann and the Origins of Homeopathy

Homeopathy was founded over 200 years ago by Samuel Hahnemann, a German doctor and chemist. His approach to medicine emphasized rapid, gentle, and permanent restoration of health. Hahnemann's work, documented in his seminal text "Organon of Medicine," laid out principles that still guide homeopathic practice today. His humane approach focused on treating the whole person rather than just the disease.

▶️ How Homeopathic Medicines Are Prepared

Homeopathic medicines are prepared through a process of potentisation. This involves diluting the original substance (often a plant, mineral, or animal product) multiple times and vigorously shaking it at each step (succussion). This process aims to enhance the therapeutic properties of the medicine while minimizing potential side effects. Each medicine undergoes "proving," where volunteers (provers) take the substance and meticulously document the symptoms experienced. This meticulous process allows a wide range of natural substances to be tested and added to the homeopathic pharmacy, all without the use of animal testing.
